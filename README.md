Foreground Subtraction of CMB Polarization with a Machine Learning Model and an Internal Linear Combination

This project contains code on my research project which worked on reductin of foregrounds and noisebias on CMB polarization data, from Plank Legacy Archive. 

This project used a developed physics-informed machince learning model and an Internal Linear Combination to reduce foregrounds in the power spectra

Abstract- 
Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) polarization measures the orientation and strength of linearly
polarized light from the CMB and has many applications that include analyzing primordial gravita-
tional waves; however, these signals contain galactic foreground and instrumental noise bias, leading
to unreliable results. This study aims to reduce foreground contamination in these signals through a
three step process using Planck’s PR3: Perform cross spectra using Half-Mission maps, develop and
utilize a physics informed AI model that is informed of the physical constraints of CMB signals to
denoise the power spectra of the signals, and use an internal linear combination that assigns weight
to different frequencies channels that results in less contaminated power spectra of CMB signals. The
results of the proposed method showed a significant reduction in noise contamination of CMB signals
measured by a 57% reduction in scatter compared to SMICA, the European Space Agency’s cleaning
product.
